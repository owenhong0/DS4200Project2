Exploratory Analysis:

The analysis of the dataset reveals insightful trends across various music genres based on key attributes. Popularity distributions show that most songs are centered around the 70s, with genres like Latino and Reggaeton having three tracks each in the highest popularity range, while Pop and Dance stand out with one song rated 100 for popularity. Regarding song duration, most tracks fall within the 2 minutes 40 seconds to 4 minutes range, with Alternative, Alt-Rock, Metal, and Rock having longer songs, some reaching up to 9 minutes, while Indie and Indie Pop feature shorter songs, often under a minute. When examining danceability, the data reveals a generally normal distribution, with genres like Alternative and Alt-Rock showing lower danceability scores, while Reggaeton and Latino music showcase the most danceable tracks, peaking around scores of 70-80. For energy, genres like Rock, Metal, Indie, and Alternative show right-skewed distributions with most songs exceeding 70 in energy, while Indie music includes 15 tracks with a perfect score of 100. Lastly, tempo trends indicate that Latino and Reggaeton music commonly have a tempo around 95 BPM, while EDM, House, and Electro music are centered around 120-130 BPM, highlighting distinct rhythmic patterns across genres.

Explanatory Analysis:

These genre suggest that the geographic and cultural distributions of Spotify users may impact their popularity ratings . Latino and reggaeton genres having multiple high rated scores for songs may be directly impacted by the number of people who listen to that genre. This means both there are large amounts of Spanish speaking users who listen to that genre but also that Spotify grouping all of those songs together may force a lack of distribution and specificity within those genres. Trends in durations seem to be reasonable as most songs fall near the 3 minute mark that usually dominates the average lengths of songs that users are used to listening to. The longer and shorter songs can represent the intro tracks to albums or unique tracks in albums that users may usually not listen to but are included in popular albums. For danceability, energy, and tempo, the data reflects reasonable suggestions that edm and house music has more energy but less danceable while latino and reggaeton offers more groovy songs that may be slower.

Popularity Score Distribution by Genre

Interactive box plot with tooltip overlay displaying top 3 songs for each genre.

Exploratory Analysis:

The popularity score distribution across genres reveals significant variations in how different music styles perform on Spotify. Pop, reggaeton, and rock genres display higher median popularity scores, with pop showing particularly tight clustering around higher values, suggesting consistent performance across tracks. Dance and latino genres exhibit the widest spread between quartiles, indicating greater variability in popularity within these categories. K-pop stands out with numerous outlier points at both lower and higher ends, reflecting a polarized reception where some tracks achieve exceptional popularity while others underperform.

Explanatory Analysis:

The clustering of multiple high-value outliers in the latin, reggaeton, and pop genres suggests these styles benefit from strong engagement from specific listener demographics. This pattern may stem from Spotify's algorithmic recommendations and playlist curation that favor certain regional preferences, similar to how we observed that genre categorization impacts popularity metrics in our previous analysis. The relatively lower median scores for alt-rock and electro could indicate either more niche audience appeal or greater competition within these saturated categories.
